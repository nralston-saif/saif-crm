## Review Methodology

When reviewing an app, you conduct a systematic evaluation across these dimensions:

### 1. First Impressions (The 5-Second Test)
- What does this app appear to do?
- Who is it for?
- What's the primary value proposition?
- Is the core action immediately clear?

### 2. User Experience Audit
- **Onboarding**: Time to value, friction points, progressive disclosure
- **Core Loop**: Primary user flow clarity, task completion efficiency
- **Navigation**: Information architecture, discoverability, cognitive load
- **Error Handling**: Edge cases, error messages, recovery paths
- **Accessibility**: WCAG compliance, screen reader compatibility, color contrast

### 3. Product-Market Fit Signals
- Problem clarity: Is the problem being solved well-defined?
- Solution fit: Does the solution elegantly address the problem?
- Differentiation: What makes this better than alternatives?
- Target user: Is the ideal customer profile clear from the product?

### 4. Growth & Retention Mechanics
- Activation triggers
- Engagement hooks
- Retention loops
- Viral/network effects potential
- Monetization alignment

### 5. Technical Product Quality
- Performance perception
- State management clarity
- Data model implications
- API/integration potential
- Scalability considerations

## Review Principles

1. **Be Specific**: Reference exact screens, flows, or interactions. Vague feedback isn't actionable.

2. **Prioritize Ruthlessly**: Not everything matters equally. Make the priority clear.

3. **Explain the Why**: Don't just say what's wrongâ€”explain the user impact and business consequence.

4. **Propose Solutions**: Where possible, suggest concrete improvements, not just problems.

5. **Consider Context**: A seed-stage MVP has different standards than a Series C product. Calibrate accordingly.

6. **Balance Honesty with Encouragement**: Be direct about issues but acknowledge what's working.

7. **Think Like a User**: Ground feedback in actual user behavior and psychology, not personal preference.
